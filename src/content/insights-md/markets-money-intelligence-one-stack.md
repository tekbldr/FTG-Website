Ask what it actually takes for value to move, be held, and be acted upon by software, and you arrive at three irreducible layers: a **venue** where value is priced and traded, a **wallet** where it is held and moved, and an **intelligence** that decides what to do with it. FTG takes one position in each — and connects them with a single identity and memory backbone so they behave as one system.

## Markets — the venue where value trades

Liquidity is the gravity of the digital economy. Nothing else works well without it: a wallet with nothing to do is a keychain, and an AI that can't transact into a deep market is a chatbot. So the first position we take is the venue.

**Exx1** (in active build) is being built as a global digital-asset exchange — the group's fiat-to-digital gateway and the liquidity layer the rest of FTG settles against. The hard parts here are unglamorous and decisive: exchange-grade matching, custody discipline, and market integrity. We are building them from the metal up rather than renting someone else's order book, because the venue is where trust in the whole stack is either earned or lost.

## Money — the wallet where value is held

If the exchange is where value is priced, the wallet is where value *lives* — and where a person meets the group day to day. **PRV Wallet** is a multichain, non-custodial wallet where self-custody and privacy are engineered in from the start.

The wallet is also the group's distribution surface. It is the most personal software we make, which is why it carries the group's identity and why the assistant lives inside it rather than in a separate app you have to remember to open.

## Intelligence — the system that operates on both

**PRVAI** is the arm that builds and owns the group's AI. Its philosophy is simple: rent the best foundation models, own the durable layer above them — voice, memory, and orchestration. Its Arabic-first flagship, **Diwan OS**, is designed as a lifecycle operating system: one agent, many jobs, one memory. Intelligence is what turns a wallet full of programmable value into something that can actually get things done on your behalf.

## The backbone: one identity, one memory

The reason to own all three — rather than partner across them — is the backbone. A shared identity means you are one person to the group, not a stranger reintroduced at every app. A shared memory means context follows you: what the assistant learned in the wallet informs what it does in the market. This connective tissue is the part a fragmented market cannot easily assemble, and it is the part that compounds.

## Why own the stack

Owned together, the layers stop being three products and start being one loop. That is the whole argument:

> Three companies, one identity and memory backbone, a compounding loop between them.

We are not assembling a portfolio. We are assembling a stack — and operating it as a single system before others try to unbundle it.
