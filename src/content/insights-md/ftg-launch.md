First Tech Group (FTG) is launching to fund, build, and operate the core infrastructure of the digital economy. We are an operator-investor: capital with conviction, a studio that ships, and owners who stay on the ground long after the cheque clears.

Most of the value in the last technology cycle accrued to whoever owned the infrastructure — the rails, the marketplaces, the identity and data layers that everything else was built on. We think the next cycle will be no different, and that the infrastructure worth owning now sits at the meeting point of two forces: money becoming programmable, and machine intelligence becoming something that can act.

## One stack, three layers

The next financial era is being rebuilt on three layers. We are taking one strategic position in each:

- **Markets — the venue where value trades.** A global digital-asset exchange, **Exx1** (in active build), designed to be the group's liquidity layer and its gateway between traditional and digital value.
- **Money — the wallet where value is held.** **PRV Wallet**, a multichain, non-custodial wallet where privacy and self-custody are engineered in from the first commit, not bolted on later.
- **Intelligence — the system that acts on both.** **PRVAI**, an Arabic-first, sovereign-grade AI arm building assistants and the durable memory layer beneath them.

Each is a standalone business with its own customers and its own reason to exist. Together they compound: liquidity makes the wallet useful, the wallet distributes the intelligence, and the intelligence deepens the markets.

## Why now, and why us

We are building for the region first. The Gulf pairs unusual regulatory clarity for digital assets and AI with a young, mobile-first, digital-native population and patient, ambitious capital — a rare combination that makes it a natural launchpad rather than a follower. We are building to a global standard second, so nothing we ship is provincial.

> We do not build features. We assemble the operating stack for how value will move, be secured, and be acted upon.
