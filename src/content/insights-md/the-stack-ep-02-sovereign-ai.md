Episode two of **The Stack** goes deep on the intelligence layer — specifically, the moment "sovereign AI" stopped being a slogan and became a set of real, shipping decisions about where models are built, where data lives, and which language they think in first. These are the show notes.

A caveat we keep on the record throughout the episode: most of the compute figures below are **announced and forward-looking**, and every "leading model" superlative is a vendor claim. We attribute, we do not endorse.

## In this episode

We trace how the Gulf moved, in roughly eighteen months, from importing AI to producing it: state-backed compute, frontier Arabic models, and a shift in what buyers require — from "does it work?" to "where does it run?"

## What we cover

- **The compute build-out.** Saudi Arabia's PIF-backed HUMAIN and its NVIDIA partnership (projected at up to 500MW over five years), and the UAE's Stargate cluster inside a 5-gigawatt Abu Dhabi campus, with a first 200MW phase expected live in 2026 — announced capacity, and why *utilization* is the number that will actually matter.
- **Arabic models grow up.** Jais 2 (70B, pretrained from scratch), Falcon-H1 Arabic, and Saudi Arabia's ALLaM — and why "built from scratch" matters more than any leaderboard ranking.
- **The residency moment.** Why data residency is shifting from a nice-to-have to a procurement requirement across regulated Gulf industries, and how domestic compute makes region-resident AI practical.
- **What "sovereign" honestly means.** The ambition versus the reality — today's regional AI still depends on globally sourced hardware and export policy, so we treat sovereignty as a direction of travel, not a finished state.
- **What it means for an operator-investor.** Why PRVAI rents the best models and owns the durable layer — voice, memory, orchestration — instead of betting the company on training a frontier model every year.

## Takeaways

1. **The region now produces frontier Arabic models, not translations.** That structural shift matters more than which model is momentarily "#1."
2. **Residency is becoming a buying requirement.** Where the model runs is now part of the purchase decision, which favors region-resident builders.
3. **Watch utilization, not megawatts.** Announced capacity is easy; energized, productive capacity is the real signal — and the honest one.

> Region-grade AI is not English AI with an accent. It is built for the language, the culture, and the law — and increasingly, built where those things live.
