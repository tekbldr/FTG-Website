Most groups are a sum. Ours is designed to be a product — in the mathematical sense, where each part multiplies the others. That design has a name inside FTG: the compounding loop. It is the difference between three companies that happen to share a logo and three companies that make each other more valuable every day.

Here is the loop, in four turns.

## Liquidity makes the wallet useful

A wallet is only as useful as what you can do with what's inside it. Deep, reliable liquidity — the job of the exchange layer — turns a wallet from a place you store value into a place you *use* it: convert, settle, earn, pay. The venue gives the wallet its verbs.

## The wallet distributes the intelligence

The wallet is the most personal, most-opened surface the group makes, which makes it the natural home for the assistant. Rather than launching intelligence as yet another app to discover, it rides in the wallet — already in the user's hand, already holding their context. Distribution is the hardest problem in consumer software; the wallet solves it for the AI.

## The intelligence deepens the markets

An assistant that can read goals and act on them generates real, intentful activity: rebalancing, paying, settling, hedging. That activity flows back to the venue as volume and demand, deepening the very liquidity that made the wallet useful in the first place. The loop closes.

## Every interaction enriches a shared memory

Underneath all three turns runs a single backbone: one identity, one memory. Each interaction — a trade, a payment, a question answered — enriches a shared context that makes the *next* interaction better across every product. The backbone is what turns a loop into a flywheel.

## Why loops beat features

Features can be copied in a quarter. Loops compound over years, and they are brutally hard to replicate because a competitor has to reproduce every layer *and* the seams between them at once. This is why we insist on owning and operating markets, money, and intelligence together rather than partnering across them: the advantage was never any single layer. It was the loop.

> Liquidity makes the wallet useful. The wallet distributes the AI. The AI deepens the markets. And every interaction enriches a shared memory.

Build one great product and you have a business. Build a loop and you have a compounding one.
